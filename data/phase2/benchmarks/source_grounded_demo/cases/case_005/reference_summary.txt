81-year-old woman from a skilled nursing facility with advanced dementia and ESRD on hemodialysis presented with urosepsis. She became hypotensive to 82/50 and missed her scheduled Tuesday dialysis session. She received a first dose of renal-dosed piperacillin-tazobactam within 1 hour of triage and fluids in cautious 500 mL boluses rather than full 30 mL/kg resuscitation because of dialysis dependence. Her DNR/DNI status is documented — do not intubate or start CPR. Her left AV fistula is her dialysis access, so no BP cuffs, IVs, or blood draws on the left arm. She needs admission to a step-down unit with nephrology for urgent dialysis.
