9-year-old girl with moderate-persistent asthma and a known peanut anaphylaxis history presented with acute wheeze and SpO2 90% on room air after peanut exposure. Her caregiver administered one IM epinephrine auto-injector 20 minutes before arrival, and her maintenance inhaled corticosteroid had been skipped for three days. In the ED she received albuterol nebulizers every 20 minutes and was started on oral prednisolone 2 mg/kg daily for a 5-day course. She must be observed for at least 4 hours for a possible biphasic anaphylactic reaction, kept strictly peanut-free, and sent home with an asthma action plan only if she remains stable.
